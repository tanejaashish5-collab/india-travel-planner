## How to use the four documents together

The documents are layered — each one builds on the last.

**If someone asks "what is Nakshiq?"** → send them the Brand Bible (01).

**If someone is designing anything Nakshiq-branded** → they need the Visual Identity doc (02) in addition to the Brand Bible.

**If you're making a launch decision or planning a campaign** → check the Launch Playbook (03) first to see if it's already covered.

**If you need a specific piece of copy right now** → open the Tactical Assets doc (04) and copy-paste.

The rule: nothing ships without being checked against the Brand Bible's voice principles and the Visual Identity's forbidden list.

## A final note on what this pack is NOT

This pack is the foundation, not the final word. It's version one of a document set that should be updated as the brand grows. Specifically:

- **The Score methodology** in the Brand Bible is a v1 specification. As you collect real-world data from users and destinations, refine the weights for each dimension. Publish the refinements openly.
- **The launch playbook** assumes a specific market context (India, April 2026). If the economy shifts, if a competitor launches, if you get press you didn't expect — adapt.
- **The content calendar** is a 30-day starter. After Week 4, you should have enough signal to rewrite it based on what's actually working.
- **The logo files** are production-ready but the brand may benefit from a real typographic audit after 6-12 months of use. If a brand designer offers to refine the wordmark, consider it — but hold the line on the "italic iq" gesture, which is the brand's single most distinctive visual element.

This is not a locked bible. It's a working document built carefully enough to ship with and flexible enough to grow with. Treat it accordingly.
