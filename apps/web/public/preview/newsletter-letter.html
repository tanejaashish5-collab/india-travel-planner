Canberra · 19 April 2026 · № 16
Friend,
The summer hasn't arrived yet, which is the entire point of this week. Temperatures in the Delhi plains have crept past 34°C; the Himalayan foothills are still holding at 10–22. The window between "uncomfortable down south" and "everywhere reliable" is precisely where you want to be.
Five places scored a clean 5/5 for this April week. All five are mountains of a different flavour — fishing valley, fort town, tea terrace, trekking river, monastery — and every one of them is emptier than it will be in May. The honest answer for the week is, pick one and go before the holidays begin.
Barot Valley, Himachal Pradesh · 1,820m · April 2026
The place I'd put at the top is Barot Valley. It's the valley Google Maps forgot to label properly — one dhaba, no ATM, and the Uhl river loud enough that you can hear it from every homestay in town. The trout festival runs through April, which is the one week of the year Barot acknowledges it has tourists.
No ATM, no hospital, no Mall Road, just the Uhl river and you.
If Barot is the full commitment, the other four picks are its quieter companions. Champawat is the Kumaoni heritage town that the Chand dynasty ruled for 800 years and every tourist has forgotten. Chaukori is tea gardens at 2,010m with the Panchachuli peaks behind — if Kausani gets the Instagram credit, Chaukori deserves the view. Tirthan Valley is what Manali was three decades ago — trout streams, no Mall Road, gateway to Great Himalayan National Park. And Ravangla is South Sikkim's rest-stop for Buddhists and tea drinkers both, with the best Kanchenjunga view outside Darjeeling.
A word on what not to do. Kolkata this week is 38°C, 82% humidity, and honest tears. The cultural Bengal you hope for exists — but an hour away, in Shantiniketan. Tagore's Visva-Bharati campus, Baul music in the fields, and none of the traffic.
NH5 Chandigarh–Shimla is clear, three-and-a-half hours if you leave early. Parwanoo and Solan for fuel. Shimla parking is scarce — the Tutikandi lot is still your best bet.
Go with confidence.
— Ashish
